Australia's International Student Story

Over two decades, international students have become one of Australia's most important economic and cultural contributors. This visualisation explores enrolment trends, source countries, visa policy shifts, and how COVID-19 reshaped the sector.

Two Decades of Growth — and One Catastrophic Pause

Australia's international education sector grew steadily from 2005 through 2019, making it one of the world's top study destinations. COVID-19 caused a dramatic collapse in 2020–21. Since reopening, enrolments have surged to record highs — but the recovery has not been equal across all sectors.

Key Takeaways

  • Higher Education dominates and is growing. By 2025 it accounts for nearly half of all international enrolments, up from 39% in 2019.
  • India is the story of the decade. Indian student arrivals in 2025 nearly matched China's for the first time — a major shift in the source country mix.
  • VET is under policy pressure. The visa grant rate has fallen from 93% to 52% over two decades — a deliberate government response to migration-motivated enrolments.
  • ELICOS has not recovered. English language enrolments remain at roughly half their 2019 peak, suggesting structural rather than temporary challenges.
  • Concentration risk remains high. China and India account for over 40% of all students. Diversification is a stated policy goal but progress is slow.
