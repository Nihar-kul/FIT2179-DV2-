Chapter 01

Growth, Collapse,
and Rebound

From 2005 to 2019, Australia built one of the world's largest international education sectors. Then the pandemic closed the borders — and enrolments fell off a cliff. What happened next surprised everyone.

Higher Education enrolments grew 47% between 2019 and 2025, reaching an all-time record of 545,259 students. But the recovery was deeply uneven. The English-language sector, ELICOS, fell 73% below its pre-pandemic peak at the 2021 low — and still hasn't fully recovered.

“ The 2021 low point wasn't just a dip — it erased a decade of sustained growth in a single year.
Chapter 02

Where the
Students Come From

China has long dominated Australia's international classrooms. But India is closing the gap fast — and in 2025, the two were almost level for the first time on record.

Together, China and India account for more than 40% of all students over two decades. That concentration is both a strength and a structural risk for the sector.

“ India is no longer catching up. In 2025, it arrived almost level with China for the first time in recorded history.
Chapter 03

The Quiet
Tightening of Visas

Getting a student visa to Australia has become measurably harder — but not equally for everyone. The data reveals a sector being deliberately reshaped by policy.

Postgraduate researchers still enjoy a 98% grant rate. But for the vocational sector, approvals collapsed from 93% in 2006 to just 52% by 2025 — a fall of 34 percentage points.

Chapter 04

An Uneven
Recovery

The collapse hit every sector. The recovery did not. By 2025, some sectors had broken records — while others sat stranded at half their pre-pandemic size.

Higher Education now accounts for nearly half of all international enrolments. ELICOS, once a vibrant entry point, remains at roughly 52% of its 2019 commencements.

Chapter 05

Where the
Students Land

Inside Australia, international students are not spread evenly. Two states — New South Wales and Victoria — host the overwhelming majority.

That concentration mirrors the location of the country's largest universities, clustered in Sydney and Melbourne.

In Summary

Five things the data makes clear

  1. 01
    Higher Education dominates and is still growing. By 2025 it makes up nearly half of all international enrolments, up from 39% in 2019.
  2. 02
    India is the story of the decade. Its 2025 arrivals nearly matched China's for the first time on record — a fundamental shift in the source mix.
  3. 03
    VET is under deliberate policy pressure. Its visa grant rate fell from 93% to 52% over two decades, targeting migration-motivated enrolments.
  4. 04
    ELICOS has not recovered. English-language enrolments remain near half their 2019 peak — a structural, not temporary, decline.
  5. 05
    Concentration risk runs deep. China and India alone account for over 40% of all students; diversification remains slow.
